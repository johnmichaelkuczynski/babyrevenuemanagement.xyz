## Full description

Why does the seat next to you on a plane cost a different price than yours? **Basic Revenue Management & Pricing Analytics** answers questions like that from the ground up — a complete, self-paced course that teaches, tutors, drills, and grades the entire subject with AI.

This is a full unit of real coursework, not a teaser. Across eight built-out topics it covers what revenue management is, willingness to pay, price elasticity, price discrimination and fences, dynamic pricing, overbooking and capacity, discounts/bundles/anchors, and how to assemble a complete pricing strategy. It's written for adults coming to the subject for the first time — graduate students, researchers, and professionals starting a new discipline — and it builds every concept in plain language, assuming no prior background in the field.

What's inside:

📚 A complete eight-topic unit — every lesson fully written, from first principles to a capstone on setting a pricing strategy
📖 Three reading depths — read any lesson Short, Medium, or Long; same ideas, the level of detail you want
💬 A section-scoped AI tutor — ask about the exact paragraph you're reading and get a grounded answer streamed back in real time
🎯 Adaptive practice — problem sets that get harder on a streak and ease off after a miss, with an explanation on every answer
✅ AI-graded coursework — two homework sets, a timed unit test, and a cumulative final, each scored with written, per-question feedback
🛡️ Two-layer integrity checking — every submission is screened by a text classifier (GPTZero) and a keystroke-pattern detector that catches AI use even when the wording is changed
📊 Live analytics — KPIs, per-topic mastery, and a recent-activity feed so you can see exactly where you stand
🧠 Diagnostic reasoning assessments — subject and general-reasoning instruments in multiple formats and lengths, takeable throughout the course

One unit, eight topics:
• 1.1 What revenue management is: why two seats cost different prices
• 1.2 Willingness to pay: the hidden ceiling in every buyer's head
• 1.3 Price elasticity: how much demand bends when the price moves
• 1.4 Price discrimination and fences: charging different people different prices
• 1.5 Dynamic pricing: prices that change with demand
• 1.6 Overbooking and capacity: selling a little more than you have
• 1.7 Discounts, bundles, and anchors: nudging what people choose
• 1.8 Setting a pricing strategy: putting it all together
